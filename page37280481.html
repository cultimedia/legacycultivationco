As high as a Georgia Pine: Strain Info

Ah, Georgia Pine. This marijuana cultivar is beloved by many for its unmistakable terpene profile and delightful effects. But what makes it so special? Let’s explore the unique attributes of this strain and discover why it stands out from similar strains.

What is Georgia Pine Cannabis?

Georgia Pine is a hybrid strain with genetics tracing back to a cross of Killer Queen G13 and William’s Wonder. These potent parents have produced a cultivar that offers an aroma like no other. Georgia Pine oozes with sweet earth pine notes along with a hint of diesel fuel that fills any room with its presence. The buds are light green with orange pistils throughout and are covered in trichomes—leaving them sticky to the touch when broken apart.

The History of Georgia Pine

The origin story of this delicious treat began with two formidable parents: Killer Queen G13 and William’s Wonder. These two cultivars were chosen for their unique attributes, which were sure to combine into a powerful hybrid.

Killer Queen G13 is a sativa-dominant strain that is believed to have originated in California. It boasts dense buds that are covered in icy resin and has a pungent aroma with aromas of lemon, pine, lavender, and mint. On the other hand, William’s Wonder is an indica-dominant cultivar that originated in Colorado and is known for its sweet flavors and relaxing effects.

The result of these two strains combining is a powerful hybrid with some truly unique characteristics. Georgia Pine offers a euphoric high along with benefits such as stress relief, improved mood, and relaxation—making it ideal for those looking to destress after a long day or manage chronic pain without getting weighed down by heavy sedative effects.

What does Georgia Pine weed look like?

Like its parents, Georgia Pine is a beautiful plant with pale green buds covered in orange pistils and a thick layer of trichomes. The buds are dense and sticky to the touch, giving them an almost waxy appearance. When broken apart, they give off a rich piney aroma that fills the room with earthy tones reminding you of a magical forest of pines.

So if you’re looking for a marjuana strain that packs a powerful punch, look no further than Georgia Pine. Its unique terpene profile and uplifting effects make it the perfect choice for those who want to feel relaxed, happy, and focused all at the same time. What does the Georgia Pine strain smell like?

The Georgia Pine cannabis strain has a unique aroma that you won’t find in any other strain. It is sweet and earthy with hints of pine and watermelon, giving it an unmistakable scent that fills the room with its presence. The buds are also known for their sticky texture and waxy appearance when broken apart—making them both potent and delicious to smell! So if you’re looking for a pungent strain that will leave your nose tingling, look no further than the Georgia Pine Strain.

What does Georgia pine weed taste like?

When smoked or vaped, Georgia Pine has a candy black licorice and gasoline flavor with hints of earthy pine and sweet watermelon that will leave your taste buds tingling. Depending on how you consume it, the Georgia Pine strain may also have some woody notes that add to its complex terpene profile and make this strain truly one-of-a-kind. So if you’re looking for a strain that tastes as good as it smells, look no further than the legendary Georgia Pine!

Georgia Pine Terpenes

Georgia Pine is well known for its unique aroma and terpene profile. The buds of this strain are covered in trichomes, which contain a variety of terpenes with unique characteristics that provide meaningful effects. The main terpenes found in Georgia Pine are carene, pulegone, and humulene, which are known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and pain relieving properties.
1

Carene

Carene is a monoterpene that is found in many plants, including pine trees and citrus fruits. It has a sweet, woody aroma and is often used in perfumes and aromatherapy. Carene is also said to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, and is sometimes used to treat respiratory disorders.
2
Pulegone
Pulegone is a terpene found in pennyroyal, a plant in the mint family. It is a potent oil that has been used as a traditional medicine for centuries. Pulegone is a natural insecticide and is also used in fragrances.
3
Humulene
Humulene is a terpene found in cannabis and hops. It has a woody, earthy aroma and is known for its anti-inflammatory and anti-cancer properties. Humulene is also thought to be responsible for the "hoppy" taste of beer.

The effects of Georgia Pine can be felt almost instantly after consumption with an uplifting cerebral high that will leave you feeling relaxed and happy while still productive and energized.
