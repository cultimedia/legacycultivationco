An introduction to Purple Gelato: Benefits and Effects

Have you ever heard of Purple Gelato? It’s a strain of cannabis that is quickly gaining popularity. This strain is a hybrid with an incredible terpene profile, high THC levels and unique effects. In this blog post, we’ll be exploring the origins, effects and medicinal uses of Purple Gelato. Read on to find out more about this amazing bud!

What is Purple Gelato?

Purple Gelato is a hybrid strain that comes from two renowned parents - GSC (f.k.a Girl Scout Cookies) and Sunset Sherbet. The result is a sweet, berry-flavored weed with strong perfectly balanced effects. This combination creates buds that are sticky, dense and covered in glistening trichomes. If you're looking for a blissfully pleasurable indica dominant hybrid strain, then look no further than Purple Gelato. This hard-hitting cannabis strain is part GSC ("Girl Scout Cookies") and part Sunset Sherbet, two of the most well known parent strains out there. With Purple Gelato, users can expect a heavy body buzz and mind high – perfect for quiet nights in or fun nights out with friends. For those looking to truly relax or de-stress, this is likely your strain of choice!

What's it like?

How does purple gelato smell and taste?

Upon introducing yourself to the intense flavors of Purple Gelato, you will discover that its aromatic yet delicate flavor profile is captivating. The intense sweetness lends a delightful surprise tartness, leaving you with a flavor that'll tantalize your taste buds for days! Purple Gelato's intense and intense aroma lingers - like good wine - rounding off the intense flavor experience in an unforgettable way.

When smoking or vaping this strain, you'll notice its pungent aroma with notes of sweet cherries and citrus zest. The taste is also sweet with hints of berry fruitiness on the exhale.

What does purple gelato look like?

Grown to perfection, these sticky buds have it all - deep purple undertones with a dark green hue, coated in trichomes that glisten with each beam of light. Every detail matters when crafting something of such beauty and quality, as the deep purple hue stands out among the rich dark green buds, providing just enough contrast to show off the luscious trichomes. When handling them, you can feel the viscosity between your fingers as you admire their potency and beauty. From a distance someone might think they are looking at gems instead of buds!

How does purple gelato weed feel?

Purple Gelato is a strain that is designed for moderate doses, as the effects can be deceptively strong. For those who are able to moderate their dosage, however, you will find yourself in quite the euphoric state. You will experience an uplifting lightness in your body, followed by blissful relaxation that leave you with sedative effects and radiating happiness. Notoriously known among cannabis connoisseurs, Purple Gelato is renowned for its intense munchies-encouraging body high.

Recreational users will appreciate the happy yet relaxed feeling they get when using this bud. It can bring a sense of euphoria that helps relieve stress while also providing creative inspiration and enhanced moods. Of course, you may experience quite the munchies as well as some negative side effects such as paranoia if consumed in excess so always use responsibly!

THC Levels

Purple Gelato is a potent strain, testing at 21-24% THC content. This makes it one of the most powerful strains available on the market to experience its mind-body effects. Despite the higher than average levels of THC, its terpene profile provides a smooth and velvety taste with a hint of berry and citrus flavors. The effects of Purple Gelato often leave users feeling euphoric, happy, and relaxed while delivering stress-relief in potent doses. However, if you're not seasoned smokers, try a milder strain.

Purple Gelato Strain Background

The Purple Gelato strain is a cross between Sunset Sherbet and Mint Girl Scout cookies. The THC level ranges from 15-25%.

Purple Gelato was created by the team at Cookie Fam Genetics. This strain is a descendant of the famous GSC (Girl Scout Cookies) strain. The Thin Mint cookies phenotype was used in this cross, which gives Purple Gelato its minty flavor.

A cross between two stellar plants, this hybrid is a unique combination of the qualities of both cannabis strains. The buds are dense and covered in trichomes, with a purple hue. The effects of this strain are cerebral and uplifting, making it perfect for daytime use.

Terpene Profile

The terpene profile is an important factor when it comes to understanding how cannabis affects the end user. Myrcene, d-limonene, linalool, beta-caryophyllene, and alpha-pinene are all common terpenes found in cannabis and each one can have different effects.
  • Myrcene
    Myrcene is the most abundant terpene in Purple Gelato, and it's also one of the most common terpenes found in cannabis. Myrcene is known for its earthy, musky aroma, and it's also been shown to have a relaxing effect. Myrcene is thought to be more of a sedative since it lowers the barrier into the mind's consciousness.
  • Limonene
    Limonene is another terpene that's found in high levels in Purple Gelato. Limonene is responsible for the citrusy smell of the strain, and it's also been shown to help with stress. d-Limonene has been linked to providing a boost in mood.
  • Linalool
    Linalool is believed to be relaxing and stress-relieving. Linalool is a less common terpene, but it's still present in Purple Gelato. Linalool has a floral, lavender-like aroma, and it's been shown to have sedative and anti-anxiety effects.
  • alpha pinene
    Alpha pinene helps increase alertness for focus and concentration.
  • beta-caryophyllene
    Beta-caryophyllene has an intense peppery smell and is found in many strains of cannabis. It's believed to be anti-inflammatory, and it may help with pain management as well.
  • For more information on terpenes check out The Science of Cannabis Terpenes or Definition of Cannabinoids and Health Benefits.
These terpenes work together to create the unique flavor and effects of Purple Gelato. It's amazing how these diverse compounds in the terpene profile of cannabis can combine to create kaleidoscopic effects on its users!

Growing Purple Gelato

Growing purple gelato weed is a delicate art form that requires finesse and finesse alone. You must know how to properly cultivate purple gelato seeds in order to bring out the purple hues without compromise; such is the beauty of cultivating this photoperiodic hybrid. Cultivating purple gelato isn't easy by any means, but when executed well your efforts will surely pay off; purple gelato is a great strain amongst so many, and being able to successfully participate in its growth should be savored by an experienced grower.

Bring out the purple hues

If you want to bring out a deep purple hue when growing the much-loved strain, Purple Gelato , you must know how. It’s all about anthocyanines, of course, as anthocyanines are responsible for the deep coloration of this particular strain. A great strain for wax and other extracts, it all starts by increasing anthocyanine production by cooling down a few degrees and raising anthocyanin-activating nutrients in the growing indoors environment. This can be done with access to good lighting and some cleverly applied stressors that don’t cause any damage; this brings it all together and gives you the purple gelato strain with its full range of purple color.

It's possible to increase the production of anthocyanines (colors giving this strain its purple tint) when lowering night temperatures. If purple gelato seeds begin growing, you may lower the evening heat at night. It'll take 10 degree Celsius between morning and night temperatures to get better results. Purple gelato flowers in about 6-10 weeks. Purple gelato is a periodic hybrid which means it's a bit more finicky than other cannabis strains, so it's important to give it the care it needs. Make sure you're growing in a warm climate with plenty of sunlight - purple gelato needs at least eight hours of sunlight per day.

Implement low-stress training (LST) techniques during the vegetative phase

LST training also improves the production during the flowering phase. The branches are gently bent and secured in order for a stable canopy. The smaller secondary buds form a dense, thin nug when exposed to light. Alternatively, you may use LST in combination with training techniques like topography or FIM. This tactic eliminates apical control and promotes lateral growing which translates into higher secondary colas for higher yield.

Regularly prune your Purple Gelato crops

A good idea is to cut down any non vital fan leaf especially in areas where airflow is blocked or exposure is low. Regular pruning can improve bud development in optimum conditions. However, cutting leaves can adversely affect crops so avoid over-doing this.

Check pH balance

Ideally, a proper pH is essential for the production of a healthy crop that absorbs nutrients as required. When planting feminized purple gelato into soil, keep pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Hydroponic and non-soil growth media require pH of between 5–6. A word of caution, worm castings can change pH levels. 

Maintain ideal temperatures and relative humidity (RH) levels

Purple gelato plants prefer moderately hot or humid environments, so keep temperatures in the range of 60° to 80° F. Relative humidity should remain around 50% during the vegetative phases but approximately 10% lower during flowering phases.

Once you have the right conditions, you'll need to be patient - it can take up to three months for purple gelato to fully mature. But when it's finally ready, you'll be rewarded with a beautiful, fragrant plant with purple buds.

Statistics for growth include:

- Flowering time: 55-64 days
- Harvest: 56 days
- Yield: Moderate
- Height: Tall 60-80 inches
- Difficulty: Intermediate
