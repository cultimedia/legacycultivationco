Complete Review | Purple Gelato Strain

Purple gelato is a universally appealing hybrid strain which gets very much attention. The Purple Gelato strain is based on a cross-breeding between Thin Mint Girl Scout cookie and Sherbet Sunset. It's an Indica-dominant hybrid and the THC level ranges from 22 to 24-percent. Expect tiny and dense buds packed with beautiful crystals from this marijuana plant, described by many as surprisingly strong even by the most experienced smoker. Experienced smokers will enjoy the strong effects, but newcomers should look for a milder strain.

What is Purple Gelato?

It's an Indica dominant hybrid strain created by blending Sunset Sherbet and Thin Mint Girl Scout Cookies. This dank bud boasts a THC level ranging from 22-25% on average and a myriad of both Indica and Sativa effects. Seasoned smokers beware – purple gelato is not for the novice consumer! Even experienced smokers are taken by surprise by the surprisingly strong effects.

Aroma and flavor

The sweet and tangy flavor profile is complemented by a floral aftertaste that intensifies upon exhale. The aroma is very similar, although with hints of fresh citrus and berries followed by sweet and earthy pine. Expect intense flavors.

Visuals

The buds are dense and covered in glistening trichomes, with a deep purple hue.

This cultivar is a hybrid of two other very popular cultivars. The purple coloration is the result of anthocyanin pigments, which are also responsible for the purple color in fruits like blueberries and grapes.

Purple gelato buds are small-sized dark green nugs with deep purple undertones.

Experience

This bud has an uplifting high that hits hard and fast, providing a euphoric mood lift before launching your body into full relaxation. As your troubles fade away, your body will be lulled into a state of deep and peaceful tranquility. Thanks to the strong relaxing effect, this cultivar is said to be perfect for treating conditions such as insomnia, chronic pain, muscle spasms or cramps, and appetite loss.

Potency

This is a hybrid with indica-dominant effects. Its THC levels can range from 22% to 25%. This makes purple gelato a heady strain that's not recommended for first-time cannabis users. This strain is ideal for treating anxiety and depression.

Purple Gelato Strain Background

The Purple Gelato strain is a cross between Sunset Sherbet and Thin Mint Girl Scout cookies. The THC level ranges from 15-25%.

Purple Gelato was created by the team at Cookie Fam Genetics. This strain is a descendant of the famous GSC (Girl Scout Cookies) strain. The Thin Mint cookies phenotype was used in this cross, which gives Purple Gelato its minty flavor.

A cross between two stellar plants, this hybrid is a unique combination of the qualities of both cannabis strains. The buds are dense and covered in trichomes, with a purple hue. The effects of this strain are cerebral and uplifting, making it perfect for daytime use.

Terpene Profile

So, what are the terpenes that give Purple Gelato its distinctive flavor and effects? Let's take a closer look.
  • Myrcene
    Myrcene is the most abundant terpene in Purple Gelato, and it's also one of the most common terpenes found in cannabis. Myrcene is known for its earthy, musky aroma, and it's also been shown to have sedative effects.
  • Limonene
    Limonene is another terpene that's found in high levels in Purple Gelato. Limonene is responsible for the citrusy smell of the strain, and it's also been shown to help with stress.
  • Linalool
    Linalool is a less common terpene, but it's still present in Purple Gelato. Linalool has a floral, lavender-like aroma, and it's been shown to have sedative and anti-anxiety effects.

    These three terpenes work together to create the unique flavor and effects of Purple Gelato.

Side effects of Purple Gelato Weed

This potent strain has a THC level of up to 25% and is known for its relaxing and euphoric effects. However, like all strains of cannabis, Purple Gelato comes with a few potential side effects.
The most common side effect of Purple Gelato is dry mouth, also known as cottonmouth. This occurs when the glands that produce saliva become inhibited, leading to a feeling of thirst. Dry mouth is a very common side effect of smoking or vaporizing cannabis and can be easily alleviated by drinking plenty of fluids.
Another common side effect of Purple Gelato is dry eyes. This occurs when the tear glands are inhibited, leading to a feeling of discomfort and itchiness. This can be alleviated by using artificial tears or wearing sunglasses.
Finally, Purple Gelato can also cause paranoia in some users. This is most likely to happen if you are new to cannabis or if you take a high dose. If you do experience paranoia, try reducing your dose or taking a break from smoking for a while.

Growing Purple Gelato

Purple gelato photoperiodic hybrid plants demand effort, experience and individualized conditions to have the best harvest possible. It is possible to grow crop in the garden or outside, though indoor setup allows more control of the environment. Warm, humid conditions and lots of light are good (imagine Mediterranean climate conditions).

Bring out the purple hues

It's possible to increase the production of anthocyanines (colors giving this strain its purple tint) when lowering night temperatures. If purple gelato seeds begin growing, you may lower the evening heat at night. It'll take 10 degree Celsius between morning and night temperatures to get better results. Purple gelato flowers in about 6-10 weeks. Purple gelato is a periodic hybrid which means it's a bit more finicky than other strains, so it's important to give it the care it needs. Make sure you're growing in a warm climate with plenty of sunlight - purple gelato needs at least eight hours of sunlight per day.

Implement low-stress training (LST) techniques during the vegetative phase

LST training also improves the production. The branches are gently bent and secured in order for a stable canopy. The smaller secondary buds form a dense, thin nug when exposed to light. Alternatively, you may use LST in combination with training techniques like topography or FIM. This tactic eliminates apical control and promotes lateral growing which translates into higher secondary colas for higher yield.

Regularly prune your Purple Gelato crops

A good idea is to cut down any non vital fan leaf especially in areas where airflow is blocked or exposure is low. Regular pruning can improve bud development in optimum conditions. However, cutting leaves can adversely affect crops so avoid over-doing this.

Check pH balance

Ideally, a proper pH is essential for the production of a healthy crop that absorbs nutrients as required. When planting feminized purple gelato into soil, keep pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Hydroponic and non-soil growth media require pH of between 5–6.

Maintain ideal temperatures and relative humidity (RH) levels

Purple gelato plants prefer moderately hot or humid environments, so keep temperatures in the range of 60° to 80° F. Relative humidity should remain around 50% during the vegetative phases but approximately 10% lower during flowering phases.

Once you have the right conditions, you'll need to be patient - it can take up to three months for purple gelato to fully mature. But when it's finally ready, you'll be rewarded with a beautiful, fragrant plant with purple buds.
